About me, Thomas J. Watson, Sr.

Founder of IBM's management style and corporate culture

My brief introduction:

I, Thomas John Watson Sr. born on February 17,1874 was the chairman as well as CEO of IBM, thus an American Businessman. I pushed company's development internationally since 1914 to 1956. I created and developed IBM's management style and corporate cultures taking references form John Henry Patterson's training at NCR. On the basis of punched card tabulating machines, I converted the organization into efficient and effective selling one. I am one of the leading industrialist made by myself. Also I was richest amongst men in my era and was also one of the world's biggest and largest salesman.

My road to success

I have gone from working in farms to being CEO and chairperson of IBM. I started from farms, from root. So, hope this can be of some sort of motivation to the readers. Some of the works I have done throughout the years chronologically were:

  1. Family farm
  2. Teaching
  3. A year's course in accounting and business
  4. $6 a week as a Bookkeeper
  5. One year later joined a traveling salesman
  6. Selling sewing machines for Wheeler and Wilson
  7. IBM
